Refresh App

App Concept - Spring 2020

A project with the Matthaei Botanical Gardens and Nichols Arboretum. A group of Graduate and Undergraduate students came together under the guidance of a Media Manager at Matthaei Botanical Gardens, to create an app that helps the Ann Arbor Community find and utilize outdoor and natural spaces.

Goal

To develop an app using ArcGIS that guides the local community through using natural spaces to benefit their physical and mental health.

Research

While we didn’t conduct any user surveying, much of our research was grounded in the content for the app. Studying the benefits of being surrounded by nature. However, if I were to restart the design process I would begin with a user survey.

Questions:

  • RQ1: How do users currently utilize the natural spaces around Ann Arbor?

  • RQ2: Would users benefit more from an educational platform or something with social elements?

I would survey students and community members with a series of questions that help answer my research questions. These responses would help guide the team in critical decisions that we ultimately made without potential user input.

Design

Using ArcGIS, our design options were limited. I took lead on the design as a sophomore, with two freshman working with me. We didn’t have enough experience to tailor the app to our specific needs. Despite these limitations, we created a functioning demo app using ArcGIS AppStudio.

Conclusion

Given we only had a semester to work on this, meeting one hour a week. There was so much more we could have accomplished. We spent much of our time curating content, mapping out the locations, researching the benefits of each of the locations. Personally, I wish the design had gone much farther. ArcGIS allows the designer to embed the map feature into any app development software. After some experimenting I realized I couldn’t dive into learning a new software fast enough. ArcGIS’s mapping is also extremely limited as far as the interaction with the map itself. The pop-up box is frustrating and cluttered, with little room for modifications. The welcome screen is an extra set. Theres not much opportunity for adding more functions or adding a social element. We all had a vision for this project, but limited time, a pandemic, and a environmental major heavy team, our dream was cut short. I wish to continue this project again in the future with more emphasis on connecting the user with a natural spot rather than telling them what to do when they get there.
